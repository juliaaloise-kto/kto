Setting the Stage: Defining Our Sprint Challenge

Before starting the five-day process, the team must align on the challenge's scope, the long-term goal we are aiming for, and the specific questions the sprint must answer.

üéØ Long-Term Goal (The Destination)

Our customers will engage with KTO daily because they'll be looking forward to an exclusive, World Cup-themed reward unlike the usual ones.

‚ùì Sprint Questions (The Obstacles)

  • What is fundamental to the experience we want to build?
  • Does the card-drawing game resonate with our customers?
  • What does the journey of redeeming rewards look like?
  • Can we create a solution that doesn't disrupt customer behavior?
  • Can an optimal solution be built in 2 quarters?

üí° Problem Statement (The Focus)

During the World Cup, innovative engagement strategies are fundamental to ensure newly acquired customers are retained after the tournament is over. A customer that logs in every day and is encouraged to explore all that KTO has to offer - Sportsbook and Casino - is more likely to remain with us. In order to do that, we can't simply inflate our current promotions, since our customers will be bombarded with new World Cup-themed content every day. Our promotions have to be exciting and unpredictable. Along the same lines, redeeming such rewards should also be a unique experience, one that is fun and straightforward, fit even for the inexperienced bettors and not disruptive for the seasoned ones.
