Introduction
H2 2026
The strategic context

The 4% iGaming GGR share target by EOY 2026 translates to R$140M monthly GGR exit-rate by December, against a Jan–Apr 2026 baseline of R$65.9M/month. Closing this requires 13.4% MoM growth, every month, July through December. H2 execution starts in July because operational capacity is consumed by the World Cup through June.

The base Product is operating against has shifted materially. Sophia data shows the typical FTD's early value has compressed since 2022 — the median D30 NGR per FTD halved (R$30 → R$15) while the mean held thanks to a fatter whale tail. This is a composition effect of modern acquisition channels, not a within-2026 deterioration. Product is operating against a base whose typical user generates less value than they used to, while the top of the curve grows. This dynamic shapes every Objective.

The question this answers

How does Product contribute to converting the H2 active base — existing actives, baseline FTDs, and WC FTDs — into the GGR exit-rate that closes the 4% gap?

Product is one of several functions contributing to the company target. Marketing, Sportsbook, Casino, and CRM all carry a significant share of the work. This strategy describes Product's contribution, not the whole company plan.

OKR vocabulary used as intended: Objectives are aspirational and qualitative; Key Results are precise and binary — either achieved or not. Objectives describe the direction Product is committed to pulling in. Key Results commit to specific measurable outcomes against that direction.
